Background Information Regarding Friable Clearance Inspections
Following completion of friable asbestos removal works undertaken by a suitably licenced Asbestos Removal Contractor, a clearance inspection must be completed by an independent LAA / a competent person. The clearance inspection includes an assessment of the following:
  • Visual inspection of the work area for asbestos dust or debris
  • Visual inspection of the adjacent area including the access and egress pathways for visible asbestos dust and debris
  • Air monitoring to ensure asbestos fibre levels are below the clearance indicator
  • Assessment of the containment and decontamination procedures
It is required that a Friable Clearance Certificate be issued on completion of a successful inspection. The issuer needs to ensure:
  • All visible asbestos residue has been removed from the work area
  • Air monitoring results are below the clearance indicator of 0.01 fibres/mL
  • The work area has been properly cleaned and decontaminated
  • All containment measures have been appropriately removed
Friable Clearance Certificate Limitations
The visual clearance inspection and air monitoring was only carried out in the locations outlined within this document. L&D did not inspect any areas of the property that fall outside of the locations listed in this certificate and therefore make no comment regarding the presence or condition of other ACM that may or may not be present. When undertaking the inspection, the LAA tries to inspect as much of the asbestos removal area as possible. However, no inspection is absolute. Should suspect ACM be identified following the inspection, works should cease until an assessment of the materials is completed.
